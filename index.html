Minesweeper

Welcome to Minesweeper, a classic puzzle game originally made in the 1960's. For more info, check it out on Wikipedia.

How to play

Every cell on the board can either be empty, or contain a mine. Your goal is to fine and open up every empty cell, without hitting any mines along the way.

  • Left clicking will open up a cell.
  • Right clicking will mark the cell. You can set either a flag or a question mark.
    • A flag is your way to mark if you think a mine is there.
    • A question mark is for your benefit, if you aren't sure what is there yet. You cannot win the game if anything is marked with a question mark.
  • If a cell shows a number after you click on it, that number is the amount of mines around the cell (horizontally, vertically, and diagonal cells all count).
  • If you click on a cell with no adjacent mines, all cells around it will be opened up automatically for you.
  • To win, all cells must either be opened up or marked with a flag.
